WAF Bypass Analysis Everything here already got through your WAF

Your WAF sits in front of IIS, which means anything you see in these logs already bypassed the WAF. The WAF never sent the request to IIS if it was going to block it, so nothing you're reading here is a "blocked attack" — it's all the stuff the WAF let through.

The 4xx responses you see in IIS aren't from the WAF either — those are application rejections (auth failures, ACL denies, missing resources, validation errors). The WAF made its decision earlier.

What we do here: classify every bypass by what rule SHOULD have caught it, explain the payload in plain English, and tell you exactly what to configure in Radware AppWall / ModSecurity / Cloudflare to stop it next time.
